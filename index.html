2023 Trip To Europe

The fond memories live on...

From Barcelona to Provance, the photos will last a lifetime!

By Amaralyn Ewey

March 20th, 2025

The summer before transferring from Howard University to Cal, my friend and I took off with just one backpack and headed off to Europe for a month.

Since we both graduated high school during covid (2020/21), we decided we would make up our grad trip in 2023.

We started in Barcelona, and while crowded, the energy of the city was electric.

The people of Barcelona were fun, inviting, and cooked the best food.

From the fresh cortados from early in the morning, to the seafood paella in the evening, Barcelona definitely won me over.

Oh, th eplaces we went (Park Güell, Costa Brava, La Sagrada Familia & more)!

A selfie I took at the bautiful gardens of Versailles, France.

Park Güell, Spain-- quite the journey to get there, but SO worth it.

And of course, El Torre Eiffel!

After we said goodbye to Spain (Barccelona & Madrid), we made our way over to France where we landed in Provance, pit-stopped through Lion, took a day trip to Marseille, and ended our trip in Paris-- ooo la la!!

While Spain was my favorite of the two, France had lots to offer! From the freshly blooming lavender fields in Provance to the beautiful blue waters of Marseille, France, i'll never forget you!

I enjoyed France, as I was able to drag my friend to all the fictional places taken place in the country (the catacombs from my favorite aedgar Allen Poe short story, The Cask of Amontilado, the Opera Garnier, where Box 5 is reserved for The Phantom of the Opera, and even Château d'lf, the prision from one of my favorite books, The Count of Monte Christo)

I'm grateful for the time I got to spend abroad and look forward to the day I get to return to enjoy the passioante Flamenco dancers and the crepe makers outside Notre Dame!
